Slot Burner Investigation

High Speed Laser Imaging and MATLAB Analysis (Below: Central jet veolocities increasing from left to right from 30m/s to 80m/s.)

The aim of this experiment was to determine the stability of a flame on this particular burner design. This information regarding the conditions which support a stable flame while minimising fuel expenditure is useful information for future R&D into incrementally improving jet engines.

Various combinations of CH4(methane)/air mixture, visible as the tall blue flame, in the central slot were shielded from the ambient air by a C2H2 (acetylene)/H2/air pilot flame, seen as the reddish flame nearer the base of the burner.

The purpose of the surrounding pilot flame was to minimise the effect of the ambient air. The two streams were varied proportionally to avoid introducing too many variables when changing the central flame speed.

The high speed camera collected data in the form of 2000 frames at 5kHz. This dataset was captured repeatedly for many different flow conditions and analysed afterwards with MATLAB.

High Speed Camera & Laser

Laser diagnostics allow investigations of the flame without disturbing the flow conditions. A laser pulse allows for the detection of the OH (hydroxyl) molecule which is a good indicator of the reaction (flame) zone.

MATLAB Image Analysis

The MATLAB Imaging Processing Toolbox was used to extract information from the large dataset collected from the laser imaging setup. A higher relative OH molecule count indicates a more active reaction zone and thus a more stable flame.

Interpreting the Results

Literature was consulted to solve issues with the chaotic fluctuation of the reaction zone (flame). The influence of the flow conditions on the strength of the flame was found.

Conclusion

Although the results were not aimed at a specific real world problem, the knowledge gained will contribute to a better understanding of combustion which in turn will aid future developments in fuel efficiency and emissions.
